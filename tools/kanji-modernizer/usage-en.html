Old-style kanji explained

Old-style kanji refers to the pre-reform forms used in printed materials and official documents until World War II, often following the Kangxi Dictionary shapes.

  • Historically used from the Meiji era through the pre-war period
  • Based on the Kangxi Dictionary system of standardized glyphs
  • Japan‚Äôs 1946 simplification introduced new forms and popularized modern shapes
  • Family names, placenames, and shrine/temple names frequently preserve the old forms
  • Unicode variation selectors (IVS) are used to distinguish subtle glyph variants
